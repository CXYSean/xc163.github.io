Boxplots for Cases per 100k

The graph on the left shows the boxplot for cases per100k under the influence of super funds and differnet kinds of fuel plants. You can select the fuel or superfund by yourself.

From the boxplots, we can see that the mean value is very close. Only geothermal has a very obvious difference in mean. There are a lot of outliers both sides. Actually, the most cases counties have no plants and plants at all. The correlation graphs below also confirm the results.

Correlation between cases and other factors

The barplot below shows the correlation between cases and superfunds and different plants. We can see that nuclear, biomass, geothermal and cogeneration haave stronger correlation than the other factors. However, some results versus our tradition thinking. In the graph, the nuclear is contradicted to the number of cases.

Correlation between trend rate and other factors

The barplot below shows the correlation between trend rates and superfunds and different plants. Because the dataset contains these years electricity production, those may reflect the trend rates of current five years. We can see that the more recent the nuclear and petcoke are established the higher the trend rates.
